Alice Creek to Lewis and Clark Pass (to Continental Divide Trail)

For vast views, spring wildflowers, and easy access to the Continental Divide, Alice Creek can't be beat. There are few places in Montana that provide such stunning, easily accessed views from the Continental Divide. The trail is relatively wide and gradual. In late April and May, wildflowers abound, beginning with purple pasque flowers and magenta creeping phlox. About halfway to the pass, the trail crosses a seasonal stream before its final sweeping ascent to the pass.For the more adventurous hikers, follow the Continental Divide Trail a short distance to the north, and then turn west to follow a trail along the spine of the ridge, passing through rocky outcroppings before descending several miles on an old mining road.
